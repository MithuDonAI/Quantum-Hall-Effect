## Overview

This repository contains a self-study project on the **Quantum Hall Effect (QHE)** based primarily on **David Tong's Quantum Hall Effect lecture notes**. The objective of this project is to develop a comprehensive understanding of the physical principles governing electron transport in two-dimensional systems under strong magnetic fields, with particular emphasis on the **Integer Quantum Hall Effect (IQHE)** and an introduction to the **Fractional Quantum Hall Effect (FQHE)**.

The report combines concepts from **quantum mechanics, condensed matter physics, topology, and linear response theory**, presenting the key ideas in a concise and structured manner suitable for undergraduate study.

---

## Topics Covered

- Classical Hall Effect
- Drude Model of Electrical Transport
- Landau Quantization
- Landau Gauge and Symmetric Gauge
- Landau Level Degeneracy
- Magnetic Length
- Integer Quantum Hall Effect
- Hall Conductivity and Hall Plateaus
- Disorder and Localization
- Chiral Edge States
- Berry Phase and Berry Curvature
- Chern Number and Topological Invariants
- Kubo Formula and Linear Response Theory
- Introduction to the Fractional Quantum Hall Effect
- Laughlin Wavefunction
- Fractional Charge and Anyons
- Applications of Quantum Hall Physics
